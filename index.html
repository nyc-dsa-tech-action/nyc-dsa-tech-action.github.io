With the onset of the COVID-19 crisis, internet access has become even more critical to New Yorkers' lives in finding employment, accessing government services, and connecting with loved ones. Internet access in New York City, however, remains expensive and slow, and barriers to access fall disproportionately along lines of race, age, disability, immigration status, and economic status.

To achieve truly equitable Internet access for our communities and ensure that all New Yorkers have the resources needed to recover from our current crisis, we cannot allow New York City to continue subsidizing the same union-busting corporations who have failed time and again to provide functional, reliable, equitable, and inexpensive internet to all New Yorkers. Instead of private Internet monopolies, we need a municipal broadband network in NYC now!

Internet is essential

  • 79% of people used the internet for their last job search.
  • Almost 50% of students say they get assignments that require the internet almost daily.
  • 86% of Americans gets news from digital devices.

Internet service in NYC is expensive, slow, and segregated

  • New Yorkers pay some of the highest fees relative to internet speed amongst big cities worldwide.
  • 46% of NYC households living in poverty do not have broadband at home.
  • 30% of Black households and 32% of Hispanic households lack home broadband, vs. 20% of White households.

Private Internet Service Providers (ISPs) don't act in the public interest

  • Private ISPs like Spectrum don’t treat their workers well: IBEW Local 3 Spectrum workers have been on strike for four years.
  • Nor their customers: in 2018, New York State fined Spectrum $174 million+ for defrauding New Yorkers.
  • Private providers can sell your web browsing history, device location information, and other sensitive data to third parties – a municipal network would explicitly prohibit such practices.

A NYC municipal broadband network will be better than our current patchwork of private ISPs

  • Nationwide, municipal broadband networks have proven to be faster, more affordable, and more transparent than private ISPs.
  • Unlike private ISPs, a municipal network will deliver fiber-to-the-home connectivity to every New Yorker regardless of ability to pay.
  • A municipal network will be democratically run and 100% staffed by local union labor.

NYC has the resources needed to build a municipal broadband network

  • We have the existing infrastructure: thousands of miles of free conduit in the Bronx and Manhattan, thousands of City owned buildings with fiber connectivity, multiple existing municipal networks, and many thousands of available utility poles are just a portion of the infrastructure available to the City.
  • We have the people power: experienced IBEW Local 3 workers ready to hit the ground running and multiple City agencies and employees with expertise in network and call-center operations and more.
  • We have the money: In the short term, the City is already spending big and giving away infrastructure to facilitate network expansion by private ISP’s – this makes sense where it addresses immediate connectivity needs but is not the long-term solution. In the medium to long term, funding may be supported by subscriber fees, state and federal funding and some borrowing. Targeted ISP taxes, low-cost infrastructure buyouts, and additional state and local legislation will all help to drive down costs for the municipal network.
